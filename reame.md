# 🎮 All According to Plan

A browser-based dystopian political strategy card game where you manage a fragile state system through propaganda, security, economic control, and elite manipulation.

> “Everything is fine. The system is stable.”

---

## 🧠 Concept

**All According to Plan** is a turn-based card strategy game inspired by board games like Arkham Horror and Eldritch Horror, combined with dystopian governance simulation.

You are the system architect of a controlled society balancing:

- 👥 People (satisfaction / loyalty / fear)
- 🏛️ Elites (satisfaction / loyalty / fear)
- 🪖 Security (satisfaction / loyalty / fear)

Your goal is not to “win” — but to maintain **stability over 25 rounds** while avoiding collapse.

---

## 🎯 Core Gameplay Loop

Each game consists of **25 rounds**.

Each round has:

### 1. PLAYER PHASE (3 actions)
You can perform up to 3 actions:

- 🃏 Play a card from your hand
- 📥 Draw a card
- 💰 Gain a resource (money / influence / authority)

---

### 2. EVENT PHASE
After actions:
- A crisis/event is triggered
- Events affect global stability
- New challenges appear dynamically

---

### 3. END OF ROUND
- Draw 1 bonus card
- Gain 1 resource
- Advance round counter

---

## 🃏 Card System

Players use a hand-based system:

- Maximum hand size: **8 cards**
- Cards are drawn from a deck
- Cards have:
  - cost (resources)
  - description
  - clear stat effects
  - optional delayed effects

### Example effects:
- People +2 satisfaction
- Elites -1 loyalty
- Security +3 fear

---

## 💰 Resources

You manage 1 main resource:

- 💵 Money

Resources are used to play powerful cards and maintain control over the system.

---

## 📊 Stats System

Each faction has 3 core stats:

### 👥 People
- Satisfaction (stability & approval)
- Loyalty (long-term control)
- Fear (instability pressure)

### 🏛️ Elites
- Satisfaction (political support)
- Loyalty (regime stability)
- Fear (internal tension)

### 🪖 Security
- Satisfaction (system efficiency)
- Loyalty (obedience level)
- Fear (overreaction risk)

---

## ⚠️ Stability System

The world reacts dynamically:

- High fear → riots, instability events
- Low loyalty → elite conspiracies
- Low satisfaction → protests & unrest

Stability is not static — it evolves each round.
